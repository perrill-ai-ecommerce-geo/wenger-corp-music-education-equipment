## 10. Glossary and terminology

- **Music education equipment:**  
  Products used to support music teaching, rehearsal, practice, storage, and performance in schools, universities, and arts programs.

- **Music chair:**  
  A chair designed for musicians and rehearsal environments, often selected for posture, comfort, durability, and ensemble use.

- **Music stand:**  
  A stand used to hold sheet music or digital materials during practice, rehearsal, and performance.

- **Instrument storage:**  
  Cabinets, racks, carts, shelving, or storage systems used to organize and protect musical instruments.

- **Choral riser:**  
  A tiered platform used to position singers at different heights during rehearsals or performances.

- **Seated riser:**  
  A tiered platform designed to accommodate seated performers, ensembles, or presentation-style setups.

- **Portable staging:**  
  Stage platforms that can be assembled, moved, reconfigured, or stored for different events or spaces.

- **StageTek:**  
  A Wenger staging product line used for portable stage and platform applications.

- **Acoustical treatment:**  
  Products or design strategies used to improve sound quality within a room by managing reflections, reverberation, or clarity.

- **Sound-isolation room:**  
  A room or enclosure designed to reduce sound transfer between the inside and outside of the space.

- **Multi-purpose performance space:**  
  A shared space such as a gym, cafeteria, commons, or auditorium used for performances, assemblies, classes, meetings, and other events.

- **Setup and teardown:**  
  The process of preparing equipment for use before an event and removing or storing it afterward.

- **Prop 28:**  
  California’s Arts and Music in Schools funding program. Wenger-related content may discuss Prop 28 in connection with music education equipment and performing arts facility planning, but eligibility should be confirmed through official guidance.
